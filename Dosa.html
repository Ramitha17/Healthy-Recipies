Onion Rava Dosa

Calories:

One Onion Rava Dosa gives 172 calories. Carbohydrates make up 89 calories, proteins account for 10 calories, and fats contribute 72 calories. This dosa provides 9% of a standard adult’s daily calorie intake (2,000 calories).

Health Benefits:

  1. A healthier & tastier alternative to regular dosa.
  2. Made from semolina (rava), rich in fiber, protein, iron, calcium, and Vitamin B6.

Ingredients:

  • 1/2 cup Sooji (Semolina/ Rava) - roasted
  • 1/2 cup Rice flour - roasted
  • 1/3 cup All Purpose Flour (Maida)
  • 2 Green Chilies - finely chopped
  • 1 cup Shallots (or onions) - finely chopped
  • 1 tbsp Black pepper corns
  • 2 sprigs Coriander Leaves - chopped
  • 1 sprig Curry leaves - torn
  • 2 cups Buttermilk
  • 1 tbsp Oil
  • 1 tsp Cumin seeds (Jeera)
  • 1 tbsp Fresh coconut - grated
  • Salt to taste

How to Make Onion Rava Dosa:

  1. In a pan, heat 1 tsp oil, add cumin seeds and roast grated coconut.
  2. In a bowl, mix rava, rice flour, maida, chilies, onions, curry leaves, coriander, peppercorns, coconut, and salt.
  3. Add buttermilk gradually while stirring to form a runny batter.
  4. Let it rest for 15 minutes (adjust consistency with water if needed).
  5. Heat a tawa, pour batter from edges to center (don’t spread manually).
  6. Drizzle oil, cook until golden & crispy on one side.
  7. Fold and serve with coconut chutney & filter coffee.
