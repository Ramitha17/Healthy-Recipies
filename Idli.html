Ragi Rava Idli

Calories:

There are 360 calories in 100g of MTR Ragi Rava Idli.

Calorie Breakdown: 18% fat, 73% carbs, 9% protein.

Health Benefits:

  1. Nutrient-Rich: Rich in carbohydrates, protein, fiber, calcium, iron, magnesium, and potassium.
  2. Gluten-Free Alternative: Perfect for those with gluten sensitivities.
  3. Bone Health: High in calcium, great for strong bones & teeth.
  4. Energy Sustainer: Provides slow-release energy, keeping you active longer.
  5. Weight Management: Low glycemic index helps control blood sugar & cravings.
  6. Versatile Usage: Can be used in porridge, dosa, idli, and baked goods.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup Ragi Flour (Finger Millet/ Nagli)
  • 1 cup Sooji (Semolina/ Rava)
  • 1 cup Curd (Dahi / Yogurt)
  • 1 inch Ginger
  • 1 teaspoon Enos Fruit Salt
  • 1 teaspoon Sesame (Gingelly) Oil
  • Seasoning:
    • 1/2 teaspoon Sesame Oil
    • 1/2 teaspoon Mustard Seeds (Rai/ Kadugu)
    • 1 teaspoon White Urad Dal (Split)
    • 1 sprig Curry Leaves - finely chopped

How to Make Ragi Rava Idli:

  1. In a bowl, mix ragi flour, sooji, curd, Enos fruit salt, oil, and salt.
  2. Gradually add water until you get a thick batter (~1.5 cups). Let it rest for 10-15 minutes.
  3. Meanwhile, prepare tadka: Heat oil, add mustard seeds & urad dal, cook until golden.
  4. Add chopped curry leaves, ginger, green chilies, saut√© briefly & let cool.
  5. Mix tadka into the batter after resting.
  6. Grease idli molds, pour batter halfway into each mold.
  7. Steam for 10 minutes on high heat.
  8. Check if done with a knife test (should come out clean).
  9. Let idlis rest for 3-4 minutes before removing.
  10. Serve hot with Tomato Chutney & Idli Milagai Podi!
