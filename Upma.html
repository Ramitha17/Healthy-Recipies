Vegetable Rava Upma

Calories:

1 cup of Vegetable Rava Upma contains 334 calories.

Macronutrient Breakdown: 68% carbs, 24% fat, 8% protein.

Health Benefits:

  1. Sustained Energy Release: The complex carbohydrates in semolina provide long-lasting energy.
  2. Easy to Digest: Light on the stomach & easy to digest with ginger & mustard seeds aiding digestion.
  3. Rich in Nutrients: Packed with vegetables like carrots, peas, and beans, providing essential vitamins & minerals.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup Sooji (Semolina/ Rava)
  • 1 Onion, finely chopped
  • 1-inch Ginger, finely chopped
  • 2 Green Chillies, finely chopped
  • 1 sprig Curry Leaves, finely chopped
  • 1 Carrot, finely chopped
  • 1/2 cup Green Beans, finely chopped
  • 1/2 teaspoon Mustard Seeds (Rai/ Kadugu)
  • 1 teaspoon White Urad Dal (Split)
  • 1/4 teaspoon Asafoetida (Hing)
  • 1 teaspoon Oil, for cooking
  • 3 tablespoons Ghee
  • 1 Lemon, juice extracted
  • 1 teaspoon Sugar
  • Salt, to taste
  • 3 1/4 cups Water

How to Make Vegetable Rava Upma:

  1. Prepare Ingredients: Chop all vegetables & keep aside.
  2. Boil Water: In a saucepan, heat 3 1/4 cups water with salt & sugar.
  3. Cook Vegetables: Add carrots & beans, let them cook for a few minutes.
  4. Tadka (Tempering): Heat oil, add mustard seeds & urad dal, allow to crackle.
  5. Add Aromatics: Stir in onions, ginger, hing, curry leaves, green chilies.
  6. Roast Rava: Add semolina, roast until aromatic.
  7. Combine with Water: Gradually pour in the hot vegetable water, stirring continuously.
  8. Cook Until Thick: Let the mixture cook for 10 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  9. Enhance Flavor: Add ghee & lemon juice, mix well.
  10. Serve Hot: Enjoy with Coconut Chutney & South Indian Filter Coffee!
